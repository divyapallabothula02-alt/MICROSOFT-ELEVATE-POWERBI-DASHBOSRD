🧠 The Story Behind This Project

This project was created to analyze business data and identify key patterns that help in decision-making. The goal was to transform raw data into meaningful insights using a Power BI dashboard so that business leaders can easily understand performance trends.

This project analyzes the Reserve Bank financial data related to assets and liabilities and presents insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard.
The process started with collecting the dataset containing information such as total assets, total liabilities, deposits, notes in circulation, and asset categories across multiple financial years. The data was first reviewed and cleaned to remove inconsistencies and ensure accurate analysis.
After preparing the data, it was imported into Power BI where different visualizations were created to better understand the financial structure. Key visuals include:
•	Total Assets and Total Liabilities comparison by financial year
•	Distribution of assets by asset category
•	Breakdown of deposits and other liabilities
•	Notes in circulation and other deposit categories
•	Trends in financial values over time
These visualizations help identify how the central bank’s assets and liabilities have evolved across years and how different categories contribute to the overall financial structure.
The dashboard allows users to easily explore financial trends and gain insights into the composition of assets, deposits, and liabilities. This project demonstrates data cleaning, transformation, visualization, and storytelling using Power BI.
The files in this repository include the dataset, dashboard visuals, and supporting materials used to build the analysis.

🔍 What This Dashboard Explores

1.	What is the total value of assets and liabilities in the RBI financial report?
2.	How have total assets and total liabilities changed over different financial years?
3.	What is the average total deposits and average Total liabilities recorded in the data?
4.	What is the distribution of assets across different asset categories such as loans, gold, and securities?
5.	How much currency (notes in circulation) is present in the financial system?
6.	What proportion of deposits comes from different deposit categories such as scheduled banks and other banks?
7.	How do total assets compare with total liabilities across years?
8.	What insights can be derived from the trend of deposits and assets over time?
